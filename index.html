Peertube

Who is behind Peertube?

PeerTube was started in 2017 and is primarily developed by the French non-profit organization Framasoft. Framasoft is known for its work on "de-googlifying the internet" by offering free and ethical alternatives to tech giant services.

How it works

Decentralized instances: Anyone can set up their own PeerTube instance, or server, creating their own video website with unique rules and content.

Federation: Instances can connect to each other through the ActivityPub protocol, allowing users on one instance to watch videos hosted on another. This creates a larger, federated network known as the "Fediverse".

Peer-to-peer (P2P) streaming: When a video goes viral, PeerTube uses WebRTC technology to enable peer-to-peer streaming. This allows viewers to share video data with each other, which reduces the bandwidth load on the hosting server.

Key features and benefits

No vendor lock-in: Creators can export and move their content between different PeerTube instances.

No ads or tracking: PeerTube instances are typically not ad-supported and do not track user data for profiling. Monetization is up to individual instance administrators or creators, who can add support buttons for donations.

Community-based moderation: Each instance has its own administration and moderation policies, fostering diverse and independent communities.

Live streaming: The platform supports live video broadcasting.

Mobile apps: Official PeerTube mobile apps are available for both Android and iOS.
